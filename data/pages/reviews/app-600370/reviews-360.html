\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHands down, BEST point 'n' click I've played since...like...always?
I would like to say Grim Fandango, but Paradigm just smashes every expectation you could have had about the genre and turns it into something better. It's just, well, the best game of its genre, full stop. Or at the very least among the best there ever where. The creativity in the beloved series Sam & Max feels like a steaming heap of garbage compared to Paradigm, guys.

The game is permeated by all kinds of cultures (sub-, pop-, daily life-, name it and you'll find it somewhere in there) and so much of this time, it so perfectly captures the zeitgeist of our current era. Honestly, it's just a piece of art, and I say this even though I wouldn't dare to venture to claim to know what art is. It just feels like art, that's how mysteriously perfect that game is. It's protagonist suffers from some kind of cancer thing, for Gods sake, and not once does it feel as if somebody was being offended during the experience. You can almost pick up the creator's insight into life and swing it around, that's how thick the wittiness glueing together the play on words is, and that he knew what he did there, up to a point where it starts to feel uncanny.

Even if you don't like point 'n' clicks, and, admittedly, over the years I've certainly grown into a grumpy, pessimistic heathen when it comes to keeping my optimism in regard to the survival of the genre, Paradigm simply is a fresh experience you shouldn't miss out on. The FRESHEST.

The memes are strong in this one.\t\t\t\t\t
