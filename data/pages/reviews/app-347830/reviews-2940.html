\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played for about 70 hours and accomplished almost all the achievements, as far as I\u2019m concerned, it is enough gathering of experiences to write this review, to begin, I hereunder state some pretty evident advantages and weaknesses of in-game features.
+Pros
+Touching story -> 3 endings.
+Well designed characters.
+Varieties in strategy both during and outside any battle.
+Reasonable training and opponents\u2019 strength.
+Moving soundtrack.
+Cute CGs.
+Management of WP, an add-on feature that cannot be found in HDN series.

-Cons
-Little amount of dungeon maps, in response to some achievements which demand players to continuously participate in the same battle with the same performance in plethora, things might get dull.
-the storyline is kind of a repetition of 2 walkthroughs

For further reference, please watch this review~ HDN RB3
Fairy Fencer F (FFF), a tactical role-playing just like Hyperdimension Neptunia (HDN), involves a game style of 3 characters in the frontline while all the other members stay in the rear, players can switch freely between units in the front and in the reserve as long as the character that is going to be replaced is alive. This switching system is what makes planning even more important, so first and foremost, a strategy needs to be fostered in order to be able to face any kinds of situations. Of course, sometimes you will meet enemies who are just too overpowered, in this case, players will have to train their characters until they reach a level which allows them to rival these hostiles.



The reason for which I keep comparing this game to HDN is actually because both games are created by Idea Factory and Compile Heart. This is why Fairy Fencer F is heavily identical to HDN series, although there are still some major changes, for players who have already experienced any rebirths, they will be able to recognize the combat system very soon, the only difference is Weapon point system which is not set in any HDN game. As for the storyline, the game is not related to HDN at all, Fairy Fencer F has a pretty classic tale of fantasy and adventure, anymore of this will spoil the story, so I\u2019ll stop here~.

For items in game, FFF does provide various weapons and other equipments, but it did not inherit the complex \u2018\u2019plan\u2019\u2019 system found in HDN, therefore, there are only a handful of dungeons, without the DLC dungeons, I won\u2019t recommend you to aim for the achievements\u2026..it will just take too long.

The general art design is pretty, so are the soundtracks, although many of them are rather classical, it does make players immerse in the story and in any battle, of course, for some, this feeling only lasts for the early period of game.

Thank you for reading this review, I hope it will prove to be helpful to you as well as giving you a vague concept of the way this game works.~
